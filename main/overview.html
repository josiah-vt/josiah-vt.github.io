Overview of internet privacy

These resources can acquaint readers new to internet privacy with a few issues associated with the topic; they serve as good reminders of what to be aware of when considering which facets of your privacy you value and want to protect.

Glenn Greenwald: Why privacy matters

This is a presentation by Glenn Greenwald, a prominent journalist, activist, and champion of online privacy and anonymity, regarding privacy and its significance. If you're not sure why privacy is something you should value, you may be interested in the ideas Greenwald discusses. The presentation is a nice introduction to some of the most frequently-discussed issues related to internet privacy.

Juan Enriquez: Your online life, permanent as a tattoo

This is another TED talk related to online anonymity, though in this case, speaker Juan Enriquez chooses to examine the consequences of our online activity becoming permanent and archived. This is a good follow-up to the above video on privacy, because it's an example of what can happen to our data if it doesn't stay private and anonymous.

Why online privacy matters — and how to protect yours (ideas.ted.com)

The article "Why online privacy matters — and how to protect yours", found on ted.ideas.com, is an informative and cautionary interview with Christopher Soghoian, another proponent of online privacy. He discusses both methods of improving one's privacy and, perhaps most importantly, stresses that privacy isn't only about valuing one's privacy or not--it's about determining which aspects of one's privacy one values, and what troube they're willing to go to in order to protect it.

privacyrights.org

privacyrights.org is a site dedicated to aiding individuals in maintaining their online privacy. This guide presents a clear and thorough introduction to a few of the potential threats to individuals' privacy and discusses methods they can employ in order to preserve their privacy. It simply describes technology-related terms and takes time to emphasize what users should and shouldn't do at the end of each section, which reminds readers of the most salient points.
