Privacy Checkup

If you've decided you care about online privacy and anonymity, you'll likely want to examine your own online presence. It's almost impossible to remember every online profile or post you've ever made, and sometimes it's not obvious that certain online habits can compromise one's privacy. The resources below can aid you in discovering which areas of your online presnce could be kept more private. As always, it's up to you to decide how much privacy you want and what information you value.

ip-check.info

This site visually displays info that your browser leaks whenever you visit any page on the web. It's a startling look at how you might inadvertently leak information solely by browsing the web. Follow this link to give the test a shot.

snoopsnoo.com

If you're a Reddit poster, you might find snoopsnoo.com to be a useful tool. This site analyzes Reddit accounts and views all of a poster's public activity. The analytics determine the user's interests, subreddits on which they most commonly post, posts that were highly popular or unpopular, and what a user spends most of their time discussing. All of this is a good reminder of what info you might accidentally surrender via seemingly innocuous posts.

Google's privacy checkup

Though it's simpler and produces less startling results than the resources above, Google's privacy checkup is a good way to be certain that you haven't inadvertently made sensitive information accessible to others. If you haven't ever used this tool before, spend a few minutes with it; it's critical to be aware of what others can see on your Google account, especially because it's connected to see many places--Gmail, YouTube, and Google Docs or Slides, just to name a few.

Assorted social media checkups

If you have any social media accounts, it's likely that they have specific privacy settings enabled. Perhaps you only want friends to be able to see what you post, for example. However, as these online services are updated and changed, these settings can also change without users' knowledge. Therefore, it's worth checking your social media accounts and their privacy settings occasionally. Doing so will keep you up-to-date with what others can view on your account. Take some time and review each of your accounts carefully; many even have privacy checkup tools that will walk you through the most salient settings.
