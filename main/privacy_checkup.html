Privacy Checkup

As you learn about internet privacy, you may be interested in knowing where you currently stand. Just how anonymous are you? How easily-invaded is your privacy at the moment? While some of this is evaluation that only you can do--considering what you announce to the world by posting on social media, for example--there are some helpful tools or writeups that can help you determine what sort of information you might be inadvertently leaking.

It's important to keep in mind that not all information leakage is inherently bad, and it's ultimately up to you what sort of information you consider private and how much work you want to do to maintain your privacy. These are just some tools to help you evaluate the current state of your online privacy.

ip-check.info

This is a website that peforms a check to see what kind of information is leaked by your browser every time you visit a web page. It's often startling how easy it is to inadvertently compromise one's privacy just by browsing the internet. Follow this link to try the test for yourself.

snoopsnoo.com

If you post on Reddit, you might be interested in the site snoopsnoo.com. This is a site that can analyze Reddit accounts and draw metadata from them. The analytics can determine the user's interests, most frequented subreddits, and best-received posts. It's a good reminder of how much personal information you might reveal through seemingly innocuous posts.

Google's privacy checkup

While it's less complex than the above tools, Google's privacy checkup is useful to ensure that you're not accidentally making personal information publicly accessible. If you've never checked your privacy settings, you should be certain to do so; it's important to know what you're broadcasting to the world.
