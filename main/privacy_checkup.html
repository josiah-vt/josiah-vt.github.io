Privacy Checkup

As you learn about internet privacy, you may want to know the current state of your privacy. Just how anonymous are you? What information might you have inadvertently surrendered? While some of this is evaluation that only you can do--considering what you post on social media, for example--there are some useful resources that can aid you in evaluating your current privacy and anonymity.

It's important to keep in mind that not all information leakage is inherently bad, and it's ultimately up to you what sort of information you consider private and how much work you want to do to maintain your privacy. These are just some aids to get you thinking about what information you value.

ip-check.info

This is a website that visually displays info leaked by your browser whenever you visit any page on the web. It's often startling how easy it is to inadvertently compromise one's privacy just by browsing the internet. Follow this link to try the test for yourself.

snoopsnoo.com

If you post on Reddit, you might be interested in the site snoopsnoo.com. This site can analyze Reddit accounts and view all of an account's public activity. The analytics can determine the user's interests, subreddits on which they most commonly post, posts that were highly popular or unpopular, and what a user spends most of their time discussing. All of this is a good reminder of what personal information you might reveal through seemingly innocuous posts.

Google's privacy checkup

While it's less complex than the above tools, Google's privacy checkup is useful to ensure that you're not accidentally making personal information publicly accessible. If you've never checked your privacy settings, you should be certain to do so; it's important to know what you're broadcasting to the world.
